Understanding which B2B opportunities thrive on Shopify

üéØ Shopify's B2B Sweet Spot

We see the strongest product-market fit with merchants who want Shopify to be their primary commerce platform ‚Äî not just a front-end layer. This means business logic, pricing, customer management, and order orchestration happen in Shopify.

  • Beauty & Personal Care
  • Industrial Supplies
  • Electronics & Gadgets
  • Home & Garden / Lifestyle
  • Sports Equipment
  • Automotive & Parts
Note: Some of these industries have vertical-specific challenges (e.g., seasonality in fashion, inventory/expiry dates in food & beverage), but these are the industries where we see the most launch and monetization success.

What Makes a Great Fit?

Understanding the difference between ideal scenarios and challenging ones helps partners identify the right opportunities

‚úÖ Good Fit
  • Shopify manages product catalog, pricing, and inventory
  • Orders created and fulfilled through Shopify
  • Customer data and relationships live in Shopify
  • ERP integration for syncing (not real-time dependency)
  • Standard B2B workflows (payment terms, company management)
  • Self-service buyer experience is priority
  • Heavy use of credit card payments
‚ö†Ô∏è Less Ideal (Not Impossible)
  • Shopify is only the front-end, ERP controls all logic
  • Real-time pricing calls to external systems
  • Heavy CPQ (Configure, Price, Quote) requirements
  • Complex B2B subscription models
  • Seasonal pre-orders/pre-bookings (common in fashion)
  • Most orders imported from external systems
  • Punchout or EDI-heavy workflows
üí° These scenarios are less ideal but not impossible. They typically require more custom development, longer implementation times, and may not leverage Shopify's full B2B capabilities.

üìä Variable Platform Fee (VPF) Logic

In addition to the base subscription, VPF is charged when payment goes through Shopify's system or when Shopify facilitates the order. Understanding which orders trigger VPF helps partners set accurate expectations.

VPF Charged
Orders That Trigger VPF
  • Credit Card payments (Shopify Payments)
  • ACH bank transfers
  • Admin-created orders with payment
  • POS transactions
  • Shopify-facilitated offline payments (check, wire transfer)
No VPF
Orders That Don't Trigger VPF
  • Imported orders from external systems
  • Offline payments for imported orders
  • Orders created entirely outside Shopify
  • Draft orders never completed
